    Subsection 1.6.2 Operation with fractions

    Subsubsection 1.6.2.1 Explaining the process of addition and subtraction of two or three unlike and mixed fractions.

    Definition 1.6.1.
    In everyday life, you often come across situations where we need to add or subtract parts of a whole—like pieces of cake, distances, or time. These parts are represented as fractions. Sometimes, we deal with mixed numbers, which are whole numbers combined with fractions, like \(2 \frac{1}{2}\) In this lesson, you’ll learn how to add and subtract mixed numbers with unlike denominators.
    Remember:
    1. Mixed fractions are numbers like \(3 \frac{2}{5}\) which means \(3\) wholes and \(\frac{2}{5}\text{.}\)
    2. Unlike fractions are fractions with different denominators, such as \(\frac{2}{5}\) and \(\frac{1}{4}\text{.}\)
    Activity 1.6.1.
    \(\textbf{Work in pairs}\)
    1. Get into pairs. Each group receives two sets of mixed numbers written on cards. Your task is to:
      Add the mixed numbers in the first set.
      Subtract the mixed numbers in the second set.
    2. Share your steps and answers with the class.
    \(\textbf{Key Takeaway}\)
    1. Always start by adding/subtracting whole numbers first.
    2. Convert unlike fractions to like fractions by finding the LCM of the denominators.
    3. Simplify your answer when possible.
    4. You may also convert mixed numbers to improper fractions for easier calculation in some cases.
    Example 1.6.2.
    Add the following:
    1. \(\displaystyle 2 \frac{2}{5}+ 1 \frac{2}{3}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 7 \frac{3}{4}+ 12 \frac{2}{7}\)

    Subsubsection 1.6.2.3 Multiplication of fractions.

    \(\textbf{(i) Multiplying a fraction by a whole number}\)
    Activity 1.6.2.
    Work in groups
    Kwabena, a baker, is excited to surprise his family with three delicious cakes. He eagerly pulls out his recipe book, only to realize he needs to calculate the sugar needed. Each cake, according to the recipe, requires a \(\frac{1}{2}\) kg of sugar. Kwabena wonders the total amount of sugar he will need in total?. Can you help Kwabena figure out how much sugar he needs to bake his surprise cakes?
    Share your calculations and the amount of sugar you determined with the rest of the class.
    Example 1.6.3.
    Work out: \(\frac{5}{7} \times 4 \)
    Solution.
    1. To multiply a proper fraction by a whole number, multiply the numerator of the fraction by the whole number and retain the same denominator.
      \begin{align*} \frac{5}{7} \times 4 \amp = \frac{5 \times 4}{7}\\ \\ \amp = \frac{20}{7} \end{align*}
      We now, convert resulting the improper fraction \(\frac{20}{7}\) into a mixed fraction.
      \begin{align*} \frac{20}{7} \amp = 2 \frac{6}{7} \\ \\ \amp = 2 \frac{6}{7} \end{align*}
    \(\textbf{(ii) Multiplying a proper fraction by a proper fraction}\)
    Activity 1.6.3.
    (a)
    Drag the slider Slide to explore to the right direction and observe what happens to the squares.
    (b)
    Click NEW FRACTIONS button on the bottom right hand side to regenerate new fractions and follow step \(\textbf{(a)}\) again.
    (c)
    Discuss with your fellow learners on your observation in this activity.
    Example 1.6.5.
    Work out: \(\frac{5}{9} \times \frac{2}{3}\)
    Solution.
    To multiply two fractions, multiply numerators and denominators separately and simplify the resulting fraction to its simplest form.
    \begin{align*} \frac{5}{9} \times \frac{2}{3} \amp = \frac{5 \times 2}{9 \times 3}\\ \\ \amp = \frac{10}{27} \end{align*}
    \(\textbf{(iii) Multiplying a fraction by a mixed number}\)
    Activity 1.6.4.
    Work in groups
    A delivery lorry set off from Tema town to Madina city, the lorry is transporting fresh farm produce, and the distance between Tema and Madina is \(80 \frac{1}{5}\) km. The lorry uses \(\frac{4}{5}\) litres of fuel per kilometre.
    How many litres of fuel does the lorry need for the whole journey?
    Share your work with learners in your class.
    Example 1.6.6.
    Work out: \(\frac{2}{5} \times 1 \frac{3}{7} \)
    Solution.
    1. First, we convert \(1 \frac{{\color{blue}3}}{{\color{red}7}}\) to improper fraction.
      \begin{align*} 1 \frac{{\color{blue}3}}{{\color{red}7}} \amp = \frac{({\color{red}7} \times 1) + {\color{blue}3}}{{\color{red}7}} \\ \\ \amp = \frac{10}{7} \end{align*}
    2. Multiply the fractions
      \begin{align*} \frac{2}{5} \times \frac{10}{7} \amp = \frac{2 \times 10}{5 \times 7}\\ \\ \amp = \frac{20}{35} \end{align*}
    3. Simplify the numerator and denominator by dividing through by \(5.\)
      \begin{align*} \frac{20}{35} \amp = \frac{4}{7}\\ \\ \amp = \frac{4}{7} \end{align*}
